Auditory Processing Disorder Test

Uses an inset box shadow for the bordered effect on the state.


1. My child cries, covers his/her ears, or runs away when I turn on the vacuum cleaner.


2. My child gets upset if anyone talks while we’re watching TV, even at a very low volume or during a commercial.


3. My child can’t focus on his homework if he can hear someone talking or playing in another room.


4. If I tell my child, “Go to your room and get your shoe.” she’ll go to his/her room and come back without his/her shoe.


5. My child seems disinterested in music.


6. My child gets stressed out when talking on the telephone — or even thinking about it


7. My child can’t focus on writing and listening at the same time, and has difficulty taking notes.


8. My child has been tested for hearing problems, and everything came out fine.


9. My child often says, “What?” even if no one is talking.


10. My child mishears me when I’m speaking; he thinks I said “bread” instead of “red”, for example.


11. My child often says sounds in the wrong order, long after his peers have stopped doing this.


12. My child seems distracted or has trouble focusing during class.


13. My child does poorly on oral tests but not written ones.


14. My child has difficulty listening to auditory information (teacher teaching) when no visual information is available.


15. Startled or distracted by loud or unexpected sounds.


16. Distracted by sounds not normally noticed by others; i.e., humming of lights or refrigerators, fans, heaters, or clocks ticking.
