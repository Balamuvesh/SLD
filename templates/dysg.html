Dysgraphia Test

Uses an inset box shadow for the bordered effect on the state.


1. Does your child hold pencils, pens, crayons, or markers awkwardly?


2. Is your child’s handwriting illegible? Is it ever so bad that even she/he can’t read it?


3. Does your child omit letters or word endings when writing quickly?


4. Does your child complain that writing or drawing hurts or makes his hand tired?


5. Does your child seem to have difficulty picking up small objects?


6. Does your child make excuses to try to get out of at-home writing assignments?


7. Does your child have trouble using scissors, buttoning clothes, or zipping zippers?


8. Does your child move in a way that does not appear fluid - do his/her opposite arms and hands ever seem to move out-of-sync with one another?


9. Does your child make spelling errors in common words when writing?


10. Does your child seem to avoid coloring or drawing?


11. Can your child talk confidently about what he knows, but resists writing it down?


12. Is your child restless when writing — jumping out of his/her seat or asking to be excused?


13. Does your child use a random assortment of letter sizes, line spaces, spaces between words, or print and cursive?


14. Does your child have difficulty following the rules of grammar when writing — but not when speaking?


15. Does your child leave out criticafl facts or details when writing?
